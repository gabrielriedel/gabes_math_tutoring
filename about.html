01 — My background

Real math depth, from someone who still uses it every day.

I recently graduated from California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo with a Bachelor of Science in Applied Mathematics, plus minors in Data Science, Computer Science, and Statistics. My coursework ran the full calculus sequence and well beyond it — differential equations, linear algebra, Bayesian statistics, and applied linear models — so I know exactly where the road leads and what foundations actually matter along the way.

Today I work as an analyst with the Seattle Mariners in their baseball projects group, building models and turning raw data into decisions people can act on. It keeps me sharp, and it's a daily reminder of something I love telling students: calculus isn't just a hoop to jump through — it's the language behind the models, rates, and predictions that run the modern world.

Math always came naturally to me, but what I enjoy most is the moment it starts coming naturally to someone else. That's the whole reason I tutor.

02 — Why I'm good at teaching it

I've spent years leading, mentoring, and explaining.

Knowing math and being able to teach it are two different skills — and I've spent a long time building the second one:

  • Camp counselor → Program Director. Over six summers at Cedars Camps I went from cabin counselor to directing a program of 300+ staff and 1,000+ campers. I ran the middle-school program for early teens, so I'm genuinely comfortable meeting younger students where they are.
  • University ambassador & presenter. As a selective Cal Poly "Poly Rep," I gave campus tours to hundreds of guests and traveled to high schools across California to present — public speaking and breaking down ideas clearly is second nature.
  • Mentor & trainer. I've hired, trained, and led teams of 20+ people, from new tour guides to analytics managers. Patience and clear explanation are the core of everything I do.

Put it together and you get a tutor who understands the material at a deep level and knows how to make it click — without ever making you feel behind.
